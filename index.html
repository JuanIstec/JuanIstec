What Are Computers?

  A computer is an electronic device that processes data and performs tasks according to instructions called programs. Computers can store, retrieve, and process information, making them essential tools in our daily lives, from work to entertainment.

 How Do Computers Work?

  Computers operate by receiving input (data or commands), processing that data using their internal components, and producing output (results or actions). The process typically involves four steps: input, processing, output, and storage. Software (programs) tells the computer how to perform specific tasks.

 The Most Important Part of a Computer

  The most important part of a computer is the Central Processing Unit (CPU). The CPU, also known as the "brain" of the computer, carries out instructions and processes data. It controls all the operations within the computer.

 Main Components of a Computer

 <ul> <li>CPU (Central Processing Unit): Executes instructions and processes data.</li> <li>RAM (Random Access Memory): Temporary memory that stores data and instructions while the computer is running.</li> <li>Motherboard: The main circuit board that connects all components.</li> <li>Storage (Hard Drive or SSD): Stores all data, software, and files.</li> <li>Power Supply: Provides electrical power to the computer.</li> <li>Input Devices: Such as keyboard and mouse, allow users to enter data.</li> <li>Output Devices: Such as monitor and printer, display or produce results.</li> <li>Graphics Card (GPU): Handles graphics and images, especially important for gaming and design.</li> </ul> </section>

Glossary

<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://www.shutterstock.com/image-vector/wifi-router-isolated-icon-wlan-600nw-2226089825.jpg" alt="Access Point"><div><span class="term-title">access point</span><div class="term-def">A device that allows wireless-equipped computers and other devices to communicate with a wired network.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://cdn-icons-png.flaticon.com/512/3064/3064197.png" alt="Authentication"><div><span class="term-title">authentication</span><div class="term-def">The process of identifying yourself and the verification that you're who you say you are. Computers where restricted information is stored may require you to enter your username and password to gain access.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://images.seeklogo.com/logo-png/51/1/bluetooth-logo-png_seeklogo-511465.png" alt="Bluetooth"><div><span class="term-title">bluetooth</span><div class="term-def">A wireless networking technology that allows users to send voice and data from one electronic device to another via radio waves.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://howevision.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/SW60511.webp" alt="Bridge"><div><span class="term-title">bridge</span><div class="term-def">A device used for connecting two Local Area Networks (LANs) or two segments of the same LAN; bridges forward packets without analyzing or re-routing them.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://support.usr.com/support/8022/8022-ug/images/wireless-connection.gif" alt="Cable Modem"><div><span class="term-title">cable modem</span><div class="term-def">A special type of modem that connects to a local cable TV line to provide a continuous connection to the Internet. Like an analog modem, a cable modem is used to send and receive data, but the difference is that transfer speeds are much faster. A 56 Kbps modem can receive data at about 53 Kbps, while a cable modem can achieve about 1.5 Mbps (about 30 times faster). Cable modems attach to a 10Base-T Ethernet card inside your computer.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/5efc3ccdb72aaa7480ec8179/673c4209db6b424cc223bcac_6246562abf5eec2b798237df_cache-memory-hierarchy.png" alt="Cache"><div><span class="term-title">cache</span><div class="term-def">A region of computer memory where frequently accessed data can be stored for rapid access; or an optional file on your hard drive where such data also can be stored. The act of storing data for fast retrieval is called "caching".</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://www.dragon1.com/images/cloud-computing-icon.png" alt="Cloud Computing"><div><span class="term-title">cloud computing</span><div class="term-def">A general term used to describe Internet services such as social networking services (e.g., Facebook and Twitter), online backup services, and applications that run within a Web browser. Cloud computing also includes computer networks that are connected over the Internet for server redundancy or cluster computing purposes.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://cdn-icons-png.flaticon.com/512/1047/1047472.png" alt="Cookie"><div><span class="term-title">cookie</span><div class="term-def">A small piece of information you may be asked to accept when connecting to certain servers via a web browser. It is used throughout your session as a means of identifying you. A cookie is specific to, and sent only to the server that generated it.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://img.freepik.com/vetores-premium/ilustracao-em-vetor-estilo-design-plano-de-chip-de-cpu-de-computador-em-fundo-amarelo_258353-842.jpg" alt="CPU"><div><span class="term-title">CPU</span><div class="term-def">Central processing unit; the part of a computer that oversees all operations and calculations.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://treinetic.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/Worlds-Famous-Cloud-Service-Providers.jpg" alt="CSP"><div><span class="term-title">CSP</span><div class="term-def">Cloud Service Provider; a business model for providing cloud services.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://www.netgen.co.za/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/SQL-Database.png" alt="Database"><div><span class="term-title">database</span><div class="term-def">A collection of information organized so that a computer application can quickly access selected information; it can be thought of as an electronic filing system. Traditional databases are organized by fields, records (a complete set of fields), and files (a collection of records). Alternatively, in a Hypertext database, any object (e.g., text, a picture, or a film) can be linked to any other object.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://www.ovhcloud.com/sites/default/files/styles/large_screens_1x/public/2019-09/Media1-2_Datacentre_transformation_with_Cloud_Solutions%402x.png" alt="Data Center"><div><span class="term-title">data center</span><div class="term-def">A data center (data centre / datacentre / datacenter) is a facility used to house computer systems and associated components, such as telecommunications and storage systems. It generally includes redundant or backup power supplies, redundant data communications connections, environmental controls (e.g., air conditioning, fire suppression) and security devices.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://www.minitool.com/images/uploads/2020/12/dhcp-meaning-1.jpg" alt="DHCP"><div><span class="term-title">DHCP</span><div class="term-def">Dynamic Host Configuration Protocol; a protocol that lets a server on a local network assign temporary IP addresses to a computer or other network devices.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://cdn-icons-png.flaticon.com/512/716/716784.png" alt="Directory"><div><span class="term-title">directory</span><div class="term-def">An area on a disk that contains files or additional divisions called "subdirectories" or "folders". Using directories helps to keep files organized into separate categories, such as by application, type, or usage.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://www.differencebetween.net/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/Difference-Between-Name-Server-and-DNS.png" alt="DNS"><div><span class="term-title">DNS</span><div class="term-def">Domain Name System; a service for accessing a networked computer by name rather than by numerical, (IP) address.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://cdn-icons-png.flaticon.com/512/3242/3242257.png" alt="Domain"><div><span class="term-title">domain</span><div class="term-def">Part of an Internet address. The network hierarchy consists of domains and subdomains. At the top are a number of major categories (e.g., com, edu, gov); next are domains within these categories (e.g., ohio-state); and then there are subdomains. The computer name is at the lowest level of the hierarchy.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://www.1kosmos.com/wp-content/uploads/encryption-hero.jpg" alt="Encryption"><div><span class="term-title">encryption</span><div class="term-def">The manipulation of data to prevent accurate interpretation by all but those for whom the data is intended.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://www.cisco.com/c/en/us/products/switches/what-is-an-ethernet-switch/jcr:content/Grid/category_atl/layout-category-atl/anchor_info.img.png/1645696132261.png" alt="Ethernet"><div><span class="term-title">Ethernet</span><div class="term-def">A popular network technology that enables data to travel at 10 megabits per second. Campus microcomputers connected to a network have Ethernet cards installed that are attached to Ethernet cabling. An Ethernet connection is often referred to as a "direct connection" and is capable of providing data transmission speeds over 500 Kbps.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://www.compuquip.com/hs-fs/hubfs/images/blog-images/types-of-firewalls.jpg?width=651&height=543&name=types-of-firewalls.jpg" alt="Firewall"><div><span class="term-title">firewall</span><div class="term-def">A method of preventing unauthorized access to or from a particular network; firewalls can be implemented in both hardware and software, or both.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://cdn-icons-png.flaticon.com/512/716/716784.png" alt="Folder"><div><span class="term-title">folder</span><div class="term-def">An area on a hard disk that contains a related set of files or alternatively, the icon that represents a directory or subdirectory.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://play-lh.googleusercontent.com/1nT-mYsLKH5EsShgoE5Wt0z0zh29YGudbbQBStr1dtmIrBbPMSMzT0huEOnazm1PSg=w600-h300-pc0xffffff-pd" alt="FTP"><div><span class="term-title">FTP</span><div class="term-def">File Transfer Protocol; a method of exchanging files between computers via the Internet. A program like WS_FTP for IBM PC or compatibles or Fetch for Macintosh is required. Files can contain documents or programs and can be ASCII text or binary data.</div></div></div>
<div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://study.com/cimages/videopreview/what-is-a-graphical-user-interface-gui-definition-components-examples_117052.jpg" alt="GUI"><div><span class="term-title">GUI</span><div class="term-def">Graphical user interface; a mouse-based system that contains icons, drop-down menus, and windows where you point and click to indicate what you want to do. <div class="glossary-term"><img src="https://www.crucial.com/content/dam/crucial/articles/for-pc-builders/how-to-wipe-a-hard-drive/hard-disk-drive-hdd.jpg.transform/large-jpg/img.jpg" alt="Hard Disk"><div><span class="term-title">hard disk</span><div class="term-def">A storage device that holds large amounts of data, usually in the range of hundreds to thousands of megabytes. "Hard disk" and "hard drive" often are used interchangeably but technically, hard drive refers to the mechanism that reads data from the disk.</div></div></div>
